# Think Biegger — PDSI Pitch Design

## Context

Will works at PDSI Contractors (labor, assists PM and QCM, ~1 year). He's launching Think Biegger, an AI automation business focused on custom automation builds for construction companies. PDSI would be his first client.

## Target

PDSI owner/CEO. Will has not met him before. The owner has already expressed interest in drywall estimating automation.

## Format

Casual one-pager handed over during a short in-person conversation. Not a formal presentation — more like "here's what I can do for you."

## Pitch Angle

Insider + free pilot. Will isn't an outside vendor guessing at problems — he's already on the team and invested in PDSI's success. The ask is low-risk: free pilot on the estimating tool, prove value, then expand.

## Structure

**Header:** Think Biegger logo + Will's name

**Intro (2-3 lines):** Who he is, what Think Biegger does, why he's starting with PDSI.

**Three solutions:**

1. **Drywall Estimating Tool** — Feed it plans, it spits out materials, labor, and costs. Faster, fewer mistakes.

2. **Bid Finder** — Scans government contract sites for opportunities matching PDSI's NAICS codes and certifications (HUBZone, DBE, etc.). Opportunities come to PDSI instead of PDSI hunting for them.

3. **File Organizer** — AI sorts, tags, and organizes everything (permits, contracts, insurance certs, jobsite photos) by project. Find anything in seconds.

**The ask:** Let me build the estimating tool for free. If it works, we keep going.

## Tone

Casual, confident, not salesy. Like talking to a boss you respect, not pitching a boardroom.

## Branding

Think Biegger logo (dark background, gold lightbulb replacing "I" in THINK, bold white text). No tagline needed on the pitch doc.
